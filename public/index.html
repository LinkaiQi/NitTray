Control Apple Display Brightness on Windows

NitTray is a free, open-source system-tray app for adjusting the brightness of Apple Studio Display and Pro Display XDR monitors from a Windows PC.

Why NitTray?

Apple Studio Display and Pro Display XDR do not include physical brightness controls. On macOS, brightness can be adjusted through System Settings, but Windows does not provide built-in controls for these displays.

NitTray adds a simple system-tray slider that communicates directly with the display over USB, allowing you to adjust brightness on Windows 10 and Windows 11.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Windows show a “Windows protected your PC” message?

NitTray is code-signed, but because it is a new application, Microsoft Defender SmartScreen may display this message the first time you launch it. Select More info, then Run anyway.

Does NitTray support the Pro Display XDR?

Yes. During initial setup, NitTray installs a lightweight WinUSB helper that enables brightness control for the Pro Display XDR.

What type of USB cable do I need?

Connect your display directly to your PC using a data-capable USB-C or Thunderbolt cable that supports USB 3.1 Gen 2 / 10 Gbps or higher. Power-only cables, or cables without the required data support, may prevent NitTray from detecting the display.
